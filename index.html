About

I am Lokesh Madhavrao, a Computer Science graduate and DevOps Engineer based in Bengaluru. I work at Circana India Private Limited, a product company, where I have spent roughly five years closer to operations with meaningful exposure to delivery through scripts, internal web applications, and automation that teams actually use during the day.

I care about clarity under pressure: when upgrades run or servers drift, people should see trustworthy state in one place—not scattered tickets and SSH sessions. Employer confidentiality matters; I describe work by technology and outcome, not proprietary names or data.

Selected highlights

Internal platforms I owned end-to-end—from backend collection to UI—used by operations stakeholders.

Linux fleet visibility

Built a web application that aggregates host health and inventory across Linux servers: memory, CPU cores, OS and application versions, and overall status—so teams can monitor the estate without repetitive manual checks.

  • Backend scripts collect structured facts from servers.
  • Web UI surfaces a single pane for operational triage.

Live upgrade observability

Delivered a site to monitor long-running unify upgrades on the backend: live status so operators and dependent teams can follow progress without guessing or opening fragile ad-hoc channels.

  • Reduced uncertainty during change windows.
  • Aligned visibility with how upgrades actually execute in the environment.

Outcomes: fewer repetitive manual tasks, faster orientation during incidents and change windows, and monitoring that matches how the organisation really runs.
